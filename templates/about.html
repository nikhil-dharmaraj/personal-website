My name is Nikhil, and I'm a sophomore at Harvard studying History & Literature (Modern World Track) and Computer Science, with a secondary degree in South Asian Studies. I grew up as a settler on stolen Ohlone land, in the Bay Area, CA, but I currently am an undergraduate student at Harvard University, on stolen Pawtucket & Massa-adchu-es-et land (gratitude to Native Land). Broadly, I'm passionate about the intersections of technology and social justice, and I've been lucky enough to explore these rich junctions through various internships, research opportunities, and advocacy projects.

Specifically, my interests lie in thinking about what it means to apply anti-imperialist perspectives to the production of artificial intelligence today; what we can do to build collective power against oppressive, carceral technological systems; how we can build technology for more revolutionary futures. Technically speaking, I enjoy working on projects that involve machine learning and, specifically, natural language processing (NLP)! Some tools I enjoy working with are Keras, React Native, JavaScript, Python, & Swift.

On campus, I'm really involved with Harvard South Asian Association (SAA) and the Harvard Task Force for Asian Progressive Advocacy & Studies (TAPAS) (where I work on the Decolonization committee)!

In my free time, I love to cook, learn languages, drink coffee, eat Chaat, ski, go on long late-night drives with my younger sister, and explore new cities. You can also find me fawning over one of my two adorable puppies here.
