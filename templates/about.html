My name is Nikhil, and I'm a sophomore at Harvard studying History & Literature (Modern World Track) and Computer Science, with a secondary degree in South Asian Studies. I grew up as a settler on stolen Ohlone land, in the Bay Area, CA, but I currently am an undergraduate student at Harvard University, on stolen Pawtucket & Massa-adchu-es-et land (gratitude to Native Land). Broadly, I'm passionate about technology and its intersections with radical movements for liberation, and I've been lucky enough to explore these rich junctions through various internships, research opportunities, and advocacy projects.

Specifically, my interests lie in thinking about what it means to disaggregate artificial intelligence from colonial paradigms of harm; what we can do to repatriate technological power into the hands of those most marginalized by surveillance systems; and how we can create radical tools to bring about more revolutionary futures. I love thinking, reading, and tinkering with anything related to machine learning and, specifically, natural language processing (NLP)! Some tools I enjoy working with are Keras, React Native, JavaScript, Python, & Swift.

On campus, I'm really involved with Harvard South Asian Association (SAA) (including our annual dazzling show, Ghungroo, for which I'm a music director and choreographer this year!); Harvard Task Force for Asian Progressive Advocacy & Studies (TAPAS) (where I work on the Decolonization committee); and the Harvard College Bhangra team :)

In my free time, I love to cook, learn languages, drink coffee, eat Chaat, ski, go on long late-night drives with my younger sister, and explore new cities. You can also find me fawning over one of my two adorable puppies here.
